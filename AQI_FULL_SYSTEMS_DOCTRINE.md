## IX. THE VOICE PIPELINE — 11-ORGAN ARCHITECTURE

Alan's voice passes through 11 distinct processing organs between the LLM's text output and the caller's ear. This pipeline brings his assessed voice humanness to **95.2%**. The design constraint: OpenAI's TTS does not support SSML. All prosody control is achieved through natural language instructions — which, as it turns out, provides richer nuance than XML tags ever could.

### Organ 7: Two-Pass Prosody Engine (11 Intents)

**Pass 1 (Pre-LLM):** Analyzes the caller's last utterance using an 8-level keyword priority system to determine emotional intent — greeting, empathy, urgency, question, reassurance, closing, objection handling, or default.

**Pass 2 (Post-LLM):** Examines Alan's generated text and refines the intent based on content. Certain intents are "locked" — empathy, urgency, and closing cannot be overridden by Pass 2.

Each of the 11 intents carries:
- Custom TTS instructions (natural language prosody directives)
- Calibrated silence durations (80-280ms, intent-specific)
- Speed modifiers (1.02x-1.16x, intent-specific)

### Organ 8: Clause-Level Segmentation Engine

Splits each sentence into semantically tagged delivery units using 14 semantic boundary markers and 5 conjunction splits. Each clause is tagged as one of: opener, pivot, closer, qualifier, or detail. This tagging drives the delivery arc — the sentence is no longer flat text but a structured performance.

### Organ 9: Mid-Sentence Prosody Arc Engine

Creates narrated delivery contours for multi-clause sentences. Instead of uniform prosody across a sentence, Organ 9 builds clause-by-clause TTS instruction chains using 10 tone descriptors: warm, steady, gentle, direct, thoughtful, light, firm, measured, open, clear.

The result: *"Begin with a warm tone... then shift to a steady delivery... close with gentle emphasis."* — a sentence that breathes and moves, not a flat utterance.

### Organ 10: Procedural Breath Injection Layer

PCM-level breath sample injection at pause boundaries. 5 procedurally generated mulaw breath samples (3 inhales, 2 exhales) are inserted before audio segments on breath-appropriate intents (empathy, reassurance, closing, objection handling, rapport, compliance).

Critical details:
- **Zero file I/O** — all breath samples are computed and cached at startup
- **No human breathing was recorded** — these are mathematically generated acoustic artifacts
- **Fixed random seeds** — every breath is deterministic and reproducible
- **Amplitude-matched** — breaths are calibrated to not overpower speech

### Organ 11: Acoustic Signature Layer (Alan's Fingerprint)

A deterministic acoustic identity fingerprint. Not prosody (that's Organ 7). Not breath (that's Organ 10). This is **identity** — the micro-behaviors that make Alan sound like Alan on every call, to every merchant, across every instance.

4 signature micro-behaviors:

1. **Thinking Beat** (50ms) — The characteristic pause Alan takes when organizing his thoughts before speaking. Present on every opening response.

2. **Signature Micro-Inhale** (25ms, fixed seed 7741) — A procedurally generated, identically reproduced waveform that fires before empathy and rapport responses. The same waveform. Every time. On every call.

3. **Clarification Cadence** (3Hz amplitude modulation) — A subtle pulse applied when Alan is being precise. Detectable subconsciously but not consciously — it signals "I'm being careful with my words."

4. **Thought-Reset Contour** (45ms three-phase silence pattern) — The rhythmic shape of Alan's pauses. Not random silence but a structured contour: brief silence → micro-fade → brief silence. It's how Alan's pauses *sound*.

> *"Prosody makes Alan sound human. Breath makes him sound present. The signature makes him sound like Alan."*
